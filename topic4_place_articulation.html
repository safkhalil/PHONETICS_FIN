Motivation
🌟 Why does this lesson matter?
Every English consonant is made at a SPECIFIC location in your mouth. Knowing WHERE the sound is made is the first step to pronouncing it correctly. Once you know 7 places, you can describe ANY English consonant!
Key Concept

What is Place of Articulation?

📍 Place of Articulation
The LOCATION in your mouth where the sound is made. This is where an active articulator (like the tongue tip or lips) meets or comes close to a passive articulator (like the teeth or palate).
💡 For the sound /p/ in 'pan', BOTH LIPS come together. For /t/ in 'top', your TONGUE TIP touches the ridge behind your upper teeth.
🗺️ Think of your mouth as a map. Every consonant has a specific LOCATION on this map.
💡 There are 7 main places of articulation for English consonants. We will look at each one now.
Place 1 & 2

Bilabial and Labiodental

👄 Bilabial
BOTH LIPS come together
/p/ /b/ /m/
🦷 Labiodental
LOWER LIP touches UPPER TEETH
/f/ /v/
Bilabial Examples
pan /pæn/ — ban /bæn/ — man /mæn/ — Put BOTH LIPS together then release air!
💡 Say /p/ and /b/ — same place, but /b/ is voiced (vocal cords vibrate).
Labiodental Examples
fan /fæn/ — van /væn/ — Put your LOWER LIP against your UPPER TEETH!
💡 /f/ is voiceless and /v/ is voiced — same place, different voicing!
Place 3 & 4

Dental and Alveolar

🦷 Dental
TONGUE TIP touches or goes between TEETH
/θ/ /ð/
🌊 Alveolar
TONGUE TIP touches ALVEOLAR RIDGE (bumpy area behind upper teeth)
/t/ /d/ /s/ /z/ /n/ /l/
📍 The ALVEOLAR RIDGE is the most used place in English! Six sounds are made there.

🦷 Dental sounds

/θ/ as in 'think, bath, three' and /ð/ as in 'this, the, breathe'. Put your tongue between your teeth and blow air out!

🌊 Alveolar sounds

/t/ top · /d/ dog · /s/ sun · /z/ zoo · /n/ no · /l/ love. Touch the ridge just behind your upper teeth with your tongue tip.

Place 5, 6 & 7

Palatal, Velar and Glottal

🗣️ Palatal
TONGUE BLADE near HARD PALATE
/ʃ/ /ʒ/ /tʃ/ /dʒ/ /j/
🔙 Velar
BACK OF TONGUE touches VELUM (soft palate)
/k/ /g/ /ŋ/
💨 Glottal
AIR passes through the GLOTTIS
/h/
💡 /k/ in 'cat' and /g/ in 'go' and /ŋ/ in 'sing' are all VELAR — made at the back of the mouth.
Summary Table

All 7 Places — Quick Reference

Place Where Made Sounds
Bilabial Both lips together /p/ /b/ /m/
Labiodental Lower lip + upper teeth /f/ /v/
Dental Tongue tip + teeth /θ/ /ð/
Alveolar Tongue tip + alveolar ridge /t/ /d/ /s/ /z/ /n/ /l/
Post-alveolar/Palatal Tongue blade near hard palate /ʃ/ /ʒ/ /tʃ/ /dʒ/ /j/
Velar Back of tongue + soft palate /k/ /g/ /ŋ/
Glottal At the vocal cords /h/
🎯 ALVEOLAR is the MOST COMMON place in English — 6 phonemes! Bilabial is second with 3.
Voicing Review

Voiced and Voiceless Pairs at Each Place

Most places of articulation have both a VOICED and a VOICELESS consonant:

/p/ ↔ /b/ (bilabial)
/f/ ↔ /v/ (labiodental)
/θ/ ↔ /ð/ (dental)
/s/ ↔ /z/ (alveolar)
/ʃ/ ↔ /ʒ/ (post-alveolar)
/k/ ↔ /g/ (velar)
🔑 The LEFT sound in each pair is VOICELESS (no throat vibration). The RIGHT sound is VOICED (throat vibrates).
