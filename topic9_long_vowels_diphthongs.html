Motivation

Why does this lesson matter?

🌟 Why does this lesson matter?
Long vowels and diphthongs help you tell apart words like 'ship' and 'sheep', 'coat' and 'cut', 'day' and 'die'. English has 8 diphthongs — more than most languages! Mastering them will make your English sound much more natural and help your listening comprehension too.
Long vs Short

Long Vowels vs Short Vowels

⏱️ Long Vowels
Vowels that are held for a LONGER duration than short vowels. In IPA, length is marked with the symbol ː (a colon-like symbol). /iː/ is the long version of the sound near /ɪ/.
💡 'sit' /sɪt/ (short /ɪ/) vs 'seat' /siːt/ (long /iː/). Feel how much longer you hold the vowel in 'seat'!
Short Vowel IPA Word Long Version IPA
Short i /ɪ/ sit Long /iː/ — seat
Short o /ɒ/ top Long /ɑː/ — car (different quality)
Short oo /ʊ/ book Long /uː/ — moon
Short u /ʌ/ cup Long /ɜː/ — bird
Short a /æ/ cat Long /ɑː/ — car
⏱️ The ː symbol means LONG. /iː/ = long 'ee'. /uː/ = long 'oo'. /ɑː/ = long 'ah'. Duration AND quality both matter!
Long Vowels 1-3

The 5 Long Vowels of English (Part 1)

/iː/
see, sheep, key, feet
/ɑː/
car, far, park, heart
/ɔː/
more, law, court, four
/iː/ — Long High Front Vowel
Tongue HIGH and FRONT, lips SPREAD. The 'ee' sound.
💡 see /siː/, sheep /ʃiːp/, key /kiː/, feet /fiːt/, she /ʃiː/
/ɑː/ — Long Low Back Vowel
Tongue LOW and BACK, mouth wide open, lips unrounded.
💡 car /kɑː/, far /fɑː/, park /pɑːk/, heart /hɑːt/, bath /bɑːθ/ (British English)
/ɔː/ — Long Mid Back Rounded Vowel
Tongue MID and BACK, lips ROUNDED.
💡 more /mɔː/, law /lɔː/, court /kɔːt/, four /fɔː/, war /wɔː/
Long Vowels 4-5

The 5 Long Vowels of English (Part 2)

/uː/
moon, blue, food
/ɜː/
bird, word, her
/uː/ — Long High Back Rounded Vowel
Tongue HIGH and BACK, lips ROUNDED and pushed forward. The long 'oo' sound.
💡 moon /muːn/, blue /bluː/, food /fuːd/, school /skuːl/, you /juː/
/ɜː/ — Long Mid Central Vowel
Tongue in the MIDDLE — neither front nor back, neither high nor low. Lips unrounded. This is the vowel in 'bird', 'word', 'her'.
💡 bird /bɜːd/, word /wɜːd/, her /hɜː/, nurse /nɜːs/, heard /hɜːd/
💡 /ɜː/ is special — it appears in many spellings: 'er' (her), 'ir' (bird), 'ur' (nurse), 'ear' (heard), 'or' (word). But always the SAME sound!
What are Diphthongs?

Diphthongs — Gliding Vowels 🎢

🎢 Diphthong
A DIPHTHONG is a vowel sound that MOVES — it starts in one vowel position and GLIDES toward another. You say TWO vowel positions in one smooth movement.
💡 Say 'day' slowly: deeee-ay! You start at /e/ and GLIDE to /ɪ/. This glide is the diphthong /eɪ/.
Simple (Monophthong) Diphthong
One fixed vowel position Two positions — starts here, moves there
/e/ in 'bed' — stays /eɪ/ in 'day' — starts at /e/, glides to /ɪ/
/ɪ/ in 'sit' — stays /ɪə/ in 'here' — starts at /ɪ/, glides to /ə/
🎢 MONO-phthong = ONE position (Greek: monos = one). DI-phthong = TWO positions (Greek: di = two). English has EIGHT diphthongs!
Closing Diphthongs

Closing Diphthongs — Moving TOWARD /ɪ/ or /ʊ/

⬆️ Closing Diphthongs
These diphthongs CLOSE — the tongue moves UP toward a high vowel (/ɪ/ or /ʊ/) from a lower starting position.
💡 They are called 'closing' because the mouth CLOSES slightly as you glide up.
Diphthong Example words Movement
/eɪ/ day, rain, cake, make /e/ → /ɪ/
/aɪ/ my, time, night, high /a/ → /ɪ/
/ɔɪ/ boy, coin, noise /ɔ/ → /ɪ/
/əʊ/ go, home, boat, know /ə/ → /ʊ/
/aʊ/ now, out, town, house /a/ → /ʊ/
💡 /eɪ/ is in 'day', 'cake', 'rain', 'eight', 'they' — many different spellings, always /eɪ/!
Centering Diphthongs

Centering Diphthongs — Moving TOWARD /ə/

➡️ Centering Diphthongs
These diphthongs move TOWARD the center of the mouth — they glide toward the SCHWA /ə/. They are called 'centering' because the tongue moves toward a central position.
💡 'here' /hɪə/ — starts at /ɪ/ and glides toward the schwa /ə/.
Diphthong Example words Movement
/ɪə/ here, ear, near, beard /ɪ/ → /ə/
/eə/ air, there, care, where /e/ → /ə/
/ʊə/ tour, pure, sure (old-fashioned in some accents) /ʊ/ → /ə/
💡 In modern British English, /ʊə/ is becoming rare — many speakers now use /ɔː/ instead. 'tour' = /tʊə/ (traditional) or /tɔː/ (modern).
