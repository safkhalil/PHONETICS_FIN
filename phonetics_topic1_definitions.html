Concept 1

What is Phonetics?

📖 Phonetics
The scientific study of speech sounds. It studies HOW we make sounds, how sounds travel as waves through the air, and how our ears hear them.
💡 Phonetics asks: How exactly does your mouth make the sound [p]?
👄

Articulatory

Studies how we PRODUCE sounds with our mouth

🎯 Our main focus this course
📊

Acoustic

Studies the physical properties of sound waves

👂

Auditory

Studies how our ears and brain HEAR sounds

Concept 2

What is Phonology?

📖 Phonology
The study of how sounds work in ONE specific language — like English. It asks: Does changing this sound change the meaning of the word?
💡 In English: changing /p/ to /b/ in 'pin' gives 'bin' — a different word!
🔬 PHONETICS 🧩 PHONOLOGY
Studies ALL human speech sounds Studies sounds in ONE language
Physical and concrete — mouth positions and sound waves Abstract and mental — rules in the mind
Uses square brackets [ ] Uses slash brackets / /
💬 Think of Phonetics as the SCIENCE of sound, and Phonology as the GRAMMAR of sound in a language.
Concept 3

The Phone and the Phoneme

[p]
Square Brackets → Phone — a real, physical sound
/p/
Slash Brackets → Phoneme — an abstract sound unit
📖 Phone
Any single speech sound — written in square brackets [ ].
💡 [p], [pʰ], [b], [t] are all phones
📖 Phoneme
The smallest sound unit in a language that can CHANGE meaning. Written in slash brackets / /.
💡 /p/ and /b/ are different phonemes: 'pin' → 'bin'
Concept 4

The Allophone

📖 Allophone
Different ways to say the SAME phoneme — they do NOT change meaning.
💡 Say 'pin' then 'spin'. The /p/ sounds slightly different each time. Both are allophones of /p/!
/p/ (phoneme)
[pʰ]
pin
Allophone 1
[p]
spin
Allophone 2
🔑 KEY TEST: If swapping two sounds changes the meaning of a word → they are DIFFERENT PHONEMES. If not → they are ALLOPHONES.
Concept 5

Minimal Pairs

📖 Minimal Pair
Two words that are EXACTLY the same except for ONE sound in the same position. That one difference changes the meaning.
💡 "pin" /pɪn/ ↔ "bin" /bɪn/ — only the first sound is different!
pin ↔ bin
cat ↔ bat
man ↔ pan
fine ↔ vine
think ↔ sink
bat ↔ bad
Concept 6

Distinctive Features

📖 Distinctive Feature
A property of a sound that makes it different from another sound. The main features are: VOICING (voiced or voiceless), PLACE (where in the mouth), and MANNER (how the airflow is shaped).
💡 /p/ and /b/ are the same EXCEPT for voicing: /p/ is voiceless, /b/ is voiced.
/p/ Feature /b/
voiceless Voicing voiced
bilabial Place bilabial
stop Manner stop
🎯 The ONE distinctive feature separating /p/ from /b/ is VOICING.
